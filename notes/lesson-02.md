# Lesson 02 — Exploring and Comparing Different LLMs

## Core Concepts

* Different AI models are optimized for different tasks
* Foundation models are large base models trained on massive datasets
* LLMs are often specialized or adapted versions of foundation models
* Proprietary models are easier to use, while open-source models provide more flexibility
* Embeddings convert text into numerical vectors for semantic understanding
* RAG connects LLMs with external or company-specific data
* Fine-tuning adapts a model to a specific domain or task

## Important Takeaways

* There is no “best” AI model for every use case
* Model selection depends on cost, speed, quality and business needs
* Prompt engineering is the simplest way to improve results
* RAG is often more practical than fine-tuning for many businesses
* AI systems become powerful when combined with workflows and external knowledge sources

## Questions

### When should RAG be preferred over fine-tuning?

RAG is often better when company data changes frequently or when there is not enough training data for fine-tuning.

### Why are embeddings important?

Embeddings allow AI systems to search and understand semantic meaning instead of exact keywords.

### Why do companies often use AI services instead of raw models?

Services provide infrastructure, security, scalability and easier deployment for production environments.
